Delectable Crispy Potato Rolls

Where ordinary wheat buns should be left at the door for these delights

Burgers, the all time treat we find ourselves going back to. But one thing that can spoil the event is a bland, stale, boring bun. It is time we put aside the disappointing wheat buns, and adopt the potato, and this recipe is going to show exactly how we can do that.

Things you need:

  • 125g warm water (86F/30C)
  • 3g or 1tsp yeast
  • 45g or 3 tbsp melted butter
  • 75g or 1/3c mashed potato
  • 35g or 2 3/4 tbsp sugar
  • 300g or 1 3/4c ap flour (11.7% protein)
  • 5g or 1tsp salt
  • egg wash (1 egg + 2 teas water, stirred)

Make that potato roll:

  1. Into the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with dough hook attachment, measure water, yeast, melted butter, mashed potato, sugar, flour, salt. Mix on low for 3 mins until combined. Increase speed to med-high and mix 5-6 more mins. Cover & allow to ferment at room temperature for 90 minutes.
  2. After that 90 mins, flip out dough onto a lightly floured work surface. Divide dough into six 100g pieces. Fold and shape each of the 6 pieces as shown @2:58. Place on a sheet tray lined with a piece of oiled parchment, cover, and proof at room temp for 45-60 min.
  3. Brush each dough ball with egg wash & load into preheated 400F/204C oven for 15min or until deep golden brown.
